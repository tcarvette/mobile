Anthony Tyler Carvette

B.S. - Information Analysis







High School

I received my high school diploma from Cranbrook Schools, a boarding school in Bloomfield Hills, Michigan.

At Cranbrook, I was a Residential Advisor in the dorms, a position where I helped new boarders through the transition of living away from home.

I was a Peer-2-Peer Facilitator. Myself and one other facilitator ran a weekly discussion where students could feel safe talking about stressful issues.

I was also a member of the Boy's Varsity Tennis Team.




College


Since enrolling in the University of Michigan in Fall 2018, I have switched from the College of LSA to the School of Information.

I am now pursuing a Bachelor's Degree of Science in Information Analysis, with a minor in Entrepreneurialship.


The BSI Program has taught me the skills and knowledge to identify and ask questions that matter to stakeholders, gather essential data to answer the

questions, find answers that are grounded in empirical evidence and present those answers in a convincing way.




Career


My first job was with ThirdCoastTennis.
I taught beginners the basics of tennis, and gave hitting sessions to advanced players looking to improve their game.
This job taught me how to deal with frustrated customers, as players would often get mad for not being able to make contact with the ball.


My second job was with the University of Michigan as an Instructional Aide for SI 206.
I had the opportunity to create chapters of the online textbook for students to use, and helped teach lecture content and lead review sessions in office hours.


I am currently working as a Leasing Professional with The Dinerstein Companies.
Here, I am responsible for managing prospect communications, follow-ups, and leasing processes in support of Sterling Arbor Blu's ongoing leasing and marketing efforts.
I am also responsible for representing the company as a source of information for prospective student residents and their parents.
This is done by providing excellent customer service and managing resident requests, concerns, and complaints in a timely manner.
